# Decorative vs. Informative Images

To determine whether an image should have a descriptive alternative text or an `alt=""`, evaluate its purpose.

## Decorative Images (`alt=""`)
An image is purely decorative if it meets **any** of the following conditions:
1. **Visual Redundancy**: The image is an illustration of adjacent text and the text alone conveys all the meaning (e.g., a generic photo of an airplane next to the text "Book your flight").
2. **Purely Aesthetic Purpose**: The image exists only to improve visual presentation (borders, decorative corners, spacers, textures).
3. **Visceral Ambiance**: "Stock" photography or filler images that do not contain information the user needs to understand to complete a task or understand the main content.
4. **Part of the Design**: Icons that accompany text but are not the sole mechanism to understand the action (e.g., a "House" icon next to the word "Home" in a menu).

## Informative / Functional Images (`alt="..."`)
These require a mandatory description:
- They provide critical data not found in the surrounding text.
- They show important processes or instructions.
- They are the sole content of a functional link or button.
